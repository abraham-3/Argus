The hundred-eyed
watchman

In Greek mythology, Argus was a superb watchman with a hundred eyes. Even when he slept, he closed only two eyes at a time, while the other ninety-eight were always wide open. Always vigilant of their surroundings.

Caught in the midst
of conflict

In the midst of a jealous conflict between Jupiter and her wife Juno, over a beautiful nymph by the name of Io, Argus was tasked to keep his eyes on the nymph by the jealous Juno, who transformed her into a white heifer.

The Skillful Mercury

Engulfed in guilt, Jupiter tasks Mercury, a skillful thief who relished the thrill and art of trickery, to steal Io away from Argus’ watchful eyes, and then free the nymph from her unjust curse.

Melodies from the shepherd

Unaware of his real identity, Argus asks the shepherd to keep him company during his tiresome watch.

The thief in disguise played slow soothing notes from his pipe to the drowsy watchman, finally making him close all hundred eyes with his notes and dream-giving wand. Allowing him to finally steal Io away.

The eternal punishment...

Upon discovering Argus’ failure to stay vigilant, Juno became enraged and deemed Argus unworthy of his hundred eyes.

The livid deity then stripped all hundred eyes off the watchman, and put them in the tail of her pet peacock, who wore them with pride ever since...

The End
